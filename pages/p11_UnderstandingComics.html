"Understanding Comics" by Scott McCloud is a groundbreaking exploration of the art and craft of comics, presented entirely in comic format. McCloud delves into the mechanics of the medium, explaining how comics use visual storytelling, spatial arrangements, and reader interaction to create meaning. He introduces key concepts like "closure," the active role of readers in interpreting panels, and "amplification through simplification," where iconic art styles engage readers more deeply. The book also examines the cultural significance of comics, their narrative potential, and the creative process behind them.
